Vikentiy, a natural marksman from a poor background, and Tatiana, a highly intelligent markswoman from an upper-middle-class family, met during sniper training in 1942. Their wartime romance, culminating in a private marriage under a sympathetic officer’s approval, was a testament to their resilience (GRU Archive: “Vikentiy and Tatiana’s partnership was as lethal as it was devoted”). After the war, they taught marksmanship and survival skills, passing these traditions to Scalpel.

Living with his grandparents, Scalpel honed his marksmanship, tracking, and concealment skills. Vikentiy noted: “The boy doesn’t shoot for sport—he calculates” (Personal Journal, 2008). Tatiana observed his growing detachment: “He loves us, but it’s buried deep. The rifle is his voice” (Letter to Wartime Comrade, 2009). His grandparents channeled his grief into disciplined training, fostering the traits that would define Scalpel.
